## 1. CONSOLIDATED ISSUES (deduplicated, ranked)

**FATAL to the headline claims as stated** (the paper cannot ship with these claims in their current form; the underlying data survives reframing):

- **F1. "ONLY gpt-5 restores author-identity" is unsupported three ways over.** [personally confirmed] (i) No cross-lab contrast was ever computed, and when computed none is significant (best t p=0.063, uncorrected); the significant-vs-null pattern is the textbook Gelman–Stern error. (ii) gpt-5's own effect does not survive Holm within its 4-lab family (0.086), its paired-t is 0.094, and its 95% CI includes zero. (iii) Even taking the effect at face value, my all-15-centroid decomposition shows two-thirds of it is generic drift toward *all* authors; the target-specific component is null (p=0.34). "Identity restoration" is not what the instrument measured. Compounding confounds: lab is confounded with tier (two flash models vs two flagships), the null arms carry an extra length instruction gpt-5/opus don't, and the labs' "effort knobs" are non-commensurable (gpt-5 graded 0→2720 rtok; opus effectively binary via summary chars; Gemini's 6× cap buys +18% actual thought; DeepSeek jumps 0→671→14,772).
- **F2. "Reasoning closes the entropy deficit universally (4/4 labs)" does not survive the obvious mean control.** [personally confirmed] fw_var is r≈0.93 with fw_mean per sample; the "restoration" is largely a rise in mean fw surprisal level. Under CV² no lab restores and Gemini significantly reverses; Gemini/DeepSeek show no relative-dispersion deficit at all. As instrumented, the bridge to the mode-collapse/entropy literature ("variance deficit closes") is not established; what is established is a joint mean+variance shift of the fw-surprisal distribution toward human levels. Additionally "universal" quietly includes gpt-5 whose own sign test is p=0.109, DeepSeek's dose is non-monotonic, and scorer-robustness is verified for only 2 of 4 labs.

**FIX-BEFORE-SUBMISSION (major):**

- **M1. Zero multiplicity control over a ~40–50-test campaign, with per-result test cherry-picking.** [confirmed for the key families] gpt-5 distance headlined on the sign test with the t dismissed; gpt-5 entropy and the June-28 contrast headlined on the t. Holm leaves only Gemini's entropy result (p=0.002) clearly standing. The authors' own foundation draft used Holm — this is a regression against their own standard.
- **M2. S1/S4 robustness gates are invalid as run.** [confirmed] GPT-2 off-by-one word mapping (≈48% fw precision per the measurement lane; my direct test confirms the mechanism) means scorer-invariance and the shuffle control never tested the fw construct; S4's own auxiliary prediction (shuffle shrinks human fw_var) failed silently; S1's coded ≥0.7 gate failed at 0.43 and was redefined post-hoc. The planned definitive 6.9B shuffle was never run.
- **M3. Dose-correlated attrition and truncation concentrated in the null labs' top-dose cells.** [confirmed counts] The heaviest reasoners were selectively truncated/excluded (deepseek_max 6 subfloor + 8 length-truncations; opus_max 5 subfloor incl. one all-thinking 0-word sample; 9/30 opus_max never thought). Lanes' sensitivity checks show no flip of the gpt-5 result (zero attrition there) but the null/negative claims for the other labs rest on damaged, selected cells, unreported.
- **M4. Model mislabeling and roster heterogeneity.** [confirmed] "DeepSeek-V4" is actually deepseek-v4-flash; the DeepSeek arm ran once on a recycled key and cannot be regenerated; arms span model vintages (gpt-5-2025-08-07 vs 2026 opus). Must be relabeled and the tier confound disclosed.
- **M5. Double-presentation of one finding as two.** [confirmed] The dose-response gpt-5 distance "result" is the identical 60 samples from the June-28 payoff. Meanwhile the genuinely new dose evidence (pooled ρ≈−0.09 to −0.16, all n.s.) is null — "dose-response" is really an endpoint contrast and should be named as such.
- **M6. Dose-correlated soft refusals in gpt-5 (0/3/5/4).** [confirmed, incl. sensitivity: sign p holds, t weakens] Must be disclosed; complicates "reasoning restores identity" interpretation (the treated conditions contain explicit non-imitation disclaimers).
- **M7. Null labs are low-power absence-of-evidence.** The n=10 sign test has 15–38% power at plausible effect rates; the null labs' CIs barely exclude a gpt-5-sized effect (Gemini's misses by 0.0008). "All NULL" language must go.
- **M8. Mechanism micro-prediction fails (unreported).** [not personally re-run; claims lane verified] Per-author Δfw_var vs Δdistance correlates the *wrong* way within gpt-5 (+0.24/+0.49 n.s.) and significantly wrong within opus (+0.83, p=0.003). The "necessary-not-sufficient / coherent lever" story has no author-level support; "necessary" was never demonstrated by any design element (n=7 correlational H3, p=0.07 at the primary scorer).
- **M9. Window-coverage and length non-compliance contaminate all cross-lab LEVEL comparisons.** gpt-5 writes 2.4–3× the requested length, so the 3000-token window scores its opening ~33% vs 70–83% for others, and within-doc placement degrades front-to-back by more than the entire dose effect. Slope claims mostly survive; every "entry 0.77 vs 0.20" style comparison does not.

## 2. VERDICT ON THE AUTHORS' TWO QUESTIONS

**(a) Is the data + methodology sound?** The *pipeline* is sound and unusually reproducible: every published number re-derives exactly from raw artifacts across three independent lanes and my own checks; the paired within-lab design is correct practice; the disclosure culture (withdrawn claims, documented deviations) is well above the norm. But the two headline *constructs* do not measure what the claims say. The identity axis mostly measures drift toward the generic published-fiction fw region (target-specific component null), and the entropy axis mostly measures mean fw-surprisal level (dispersion claim reverses under mean control). On top of that, the cross-lab differential claim was never tested and fails when tested, nothing is multiplicity-corrected, and both robustness gates ran on a broken second-scorer token mapping. **Not submittable as framed.** What genuinely survives, right now: (1) the instrument + its positive control; (2) a within-gpt-5 endpoint movement toward the human-fiction fw region (uncorrected sign p=0.02, robust to refusal- and attrition-sensitivity); (3) a real 4/4-lab shift of the fw-surprisal *distribution* (mean and variance jointly) toward human levels, with Gemini's the only Holm-surviving cell.

**(b) New research or amateur regurgitation?** Neither. **Solid-but-incremental, with two genuinely unoccupied niches.** Per the novelty lane's verified sweep: the length-matched per-author envelope instrument with a same-author re-entry positive control has no direct precedent (nearest misses: Eder's Rolling Delta, the impostors/PAN calibration line, Sawant 2026), and no published work manipulates vendor reasoning knobs against stylometric identity/fw-surprisal axes — first-mover credit is real (the nearest neighbor, Thennal & Hatzel arXiv 2606.17350, is uncited and must be reconciled). But the headline conclusions are well-trod: LLMs failing fw-level author imitation is established five times over (Mikros 2025; Wang et al. EMNLP'25; Zeng & Nini 2026; Reinhart PNAS 2025; Sawant 2026), and the "two-axis decoupling" is the mirror image of separability already documented by Jemama & Kumar 2025 — a paper the draft cites without connecting. "Only gpt-5" is a checkpoint-bound leaderboard fact (one model per lab, two of them flash-tier, non-equivalent knobs) that will date within a release cycle. This is not amateur work — the epistemics and reproducibility are strong — but the durable contribution is the **instrument and the dose-response method**, not the mechanism claims currently billed as the findings.
