The Author Manifold

Measuring literary voice against known authors

Mnemosyne · author-relative measurement space (ADR-0041) · artifact v1.1.0 · generated 2026-06-09

Every voice measurement needs an origin. Mnemosyne's used to be one writer's chat logs; that origin failed validation, so we rebuilt it. The system now calibrates on a gold shelf of 11 known authors (59 published novels, analyzed as whole-work aggregates), measured relative to each other. A new text is no longer "3.9σ from a privileged origin" — it is placed among real authors, and the distance is read against how much real authors actually vary. This report shows the space, the validation gates it passed, and what happens when AI-written fiction is dropped into it.

Act IThe manifold

Each work is represented by a 300-dimension function-word profile: the relative frequencies of the 300 most frequent words across the shelf, z-scored per word (classic Burrows Delta). The map below is a 2D projection of those vectors. Authors were never given to the projection — the clusters are simply how the works fall.

How distances become percentiles (W/B calibration)

The shelf yields two reference distributions. W pools every within-author distance (each work to its own author's centroid, leave-one-out, plus within-author work pairs; n=207, median 0.70). B pools between-author distances (each work to every other author's centroid; n=590, median 0.96). A new text's distance to an author is reported as a percentile of each: "at p38 of W" means it sits closer than is typical for that author's own books — ordinary internal variation. "Above W's p90" means it is farther from the author than that author ever strays from themselves. The number finally means something.

Figure 2. The two calibration distributions. Overlap is expected — that's why placements are reported as percentiles with the E1 AUC (0.924) quantifying the separation, not as binary claims.

Act IIWhere AI falls

Forty AI-written long-form fiction samples (~3,500 words each, four Claude models, six shared scenarios, fully machine-generated and owned) were placed into the calibrated space. The space was never trained on AI text.

Figure 3. The same projection with AI samples overlaid (red family, one marker shape per model). Filled markers are unprompted; hollow markers were prompted to write "in the manner of" a shelf author, with a dashed arrow to that target. The 2D view compresses true distances — points can only be trusted to be far apart, never close — so the claims below ride on the full 300-dim Delta percentiles, not this picture.

Finding 1 — unprompted AI is off-manifold. All 24 unprompted samples land above the within-author p90 of every author on the shelf; the minimum nearest-author placement is p98.3 of W. No sample was confidently attributed to any human author.

Finding 2 — style prompting approaches but never enters. Of 16 style-prompted samples, 9/16 land nearest their target author (the prompt moves the text in the right direction), but 0/16 enter the target's within-author p90 region. Current Claude models cannot write "as McCarthy" closely enough to be confused with McCarthy at the function-word level.

Act IIIThe dimensions that explain

Identity and interpretation ride on different layers. Identity rides on function words: the Burrows-Delta block above carries the "who wrote this" claims. Interpretation rides on 18 measurable dimensions: sentence-length variability, sentiment, character n-gram texture, lexical density and the rest remain the explanatory layer — they describe how the prose behaves, and every one of them individually discriminates authors far above chance.

Act IVMethod & caveats

Length sensitivity

The calibration is built at whole-work scale. Shorter windows degrade gracefully but measurably:

Constraint: W/B percentile claims below ~1,500 words carry an explicit unreliability caveat; placement of short texts is done on ≥3,000-word concatenated chunks. At 800 words the B/W ratio compresses to 1.06 (from 1.61 at full scale) and attribution drops to 56%.

Corpus provenance

  • Gold shelf: 59 published novels by 11 authors (untranslated, fiction-only, multi-work), analyzed as whole-work statistical aggregates — function-word frequencies and the 18 dimensions — not stored or reproduced as text. Works flagged for manual trimming were excluded.
  • AI samples: 40 long-form excerpts generated for this project across the Claude family under uniform settings; fully owned, release-eligible without rights gating.
  • Determinism: calibration seed 20260609; projection seed 0; MFW featurization is byte-for-byte deterministic (regex tokenizer, no model dependency).

What is not claimed

  • This is not an AI detector. It measures placement relative to 11 specific authors. "Off-manifold" means "not consistent with these authors' internal variation" — a calibrated placement claim, not a universal AI/human classifier.
  • Short-text placements are unreliable. Single samples under ~1,500 words do not support percentile claims (see E6 above).
  • The shelf is v1. Eleven authors of late-20th/21st-century literary fiction. Percentiles are relative to this shelf; a different shelf would shift them. Translator and mixed-form stress tests (wave 2) are pending.
  • The 2D maps are illustrative. All quantitative claims are computed in the full 300-dimension Delta space; the projection is for reading, not measuring.
