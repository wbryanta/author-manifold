## `data/ai-longform/` — AI-generated long-form fiction corpus — CC0 1.0

1,072 manifest records of machine-generated literary fiction (~3,500-word
target) from 8 models (Claude Fable 5 / Opus 4.8 / Sonnet 4.6 / Haiku 4.5,
GPT-5, GPT-5-mini, Gemma 4 26B, Qwen 3.6 35B) under matched scenarios and
five conditions (unprompted / style-prompted / paraphrase / exemplar /
completion), with a full generation manifest (`manifest.jsonl`). All
samples were generated by the repository owner. The prompts' source
materials do not ship: the exemplar condition's in-context excerpts and
the completion condition's source openings (drawn from in-copyright
contemporary novels) are excluded from this release.

Honest scope note on the sample texts themselves: because the exemplar and
completion conditions expose models to real in-copyright prose (and models
memorize), a small number of samples contain incidental short verbatim
echoes of in-copyright text — our audit found 4 of 1,072 samples with runs
of ≤30 words (one of which is itself a traditional song quoted as an
epigraph in the source novel). No passage of substance is reproduced
anywhere in the corpus, and no sample reproduces a source prompt. The
generated text is dedicated to the public domain under
[CC0 1.0 Universal](https://creativecommons.org/publicdomain/zero/1.0/);
CC0 applies to the machine-generated expression and cannot and does not
launder any third party's rights in incidentally echoed material.

The corpus is published for measurement research, not as creative work.

## `data/ai_baselines/` — per-sample D18 stylometric baselines — CC0 1.0

Derived numeric feature files (18 scalar stylometric dimensions per sample)
computed from `data/ai-longform/`. CC0 1.0, same as the corpus.

## `data/pd_shelf/` — public-domain calibration shelf — Public Domain

35 novels by 9 authors, all first published before 1930 and **public domain
in the United States**. Plain-text editions from Project Gutenberg
(https://www.gutenberg.org), trimmed to body-only prose by
`tools/build_pd_shelf.py` (deterministic; offsets recorded in
`data/pd_manifest.yaml`). Per-work Gutenberg eBook numbers are recorded in
each file's `# Source:` header and in `data/pd_shelf/pd_shelf_inventory.json`.

The Project Gutenberg trademark license applies to the *trademark*, not the
texts; the texts themselves are public domain. Gutenberg boilerplate has
been removed, so these files are plain public-domain text with provenance
headers. If you redistribute, retaining the provenance headers is requested
as a courtesy.

If you are outside the United States, check the copyright status of
pre-1930 works in your jurisdiction.

## `data/pd_work_baselines/` and `data/artifacts/` — derived aggregates

Per-work baseline JSONs and author-space artifacts contain only **aggregate
statistics**: 18 scalar stylometric dimensions per work and z-scored
relative frequencies of the 300 most frequent words. No passages of any
source text are reproduced.

- `author_space_pd_v1.json` is derived entirely from the public-domain
  shelf above.
- `author_space_v1_wave2.json` / `author_space_v1_wave2_fwonly.json` are
  derived from 78 in-copyright contemporary novels (15 authors) that are
  **not** included in this repository. What ships is the measurement
  artifact only: per-work z-scored frequency vectors of common words plus
  scalar dimension values — aggregate statistics, not reproductions of the
  works. Work titles and relative build paths are retained as provenance;
  the referenced text files are not part of this release. To rebuild these
  artifacts you must supply your own copies of the works.
