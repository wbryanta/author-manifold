They were at the pump by first light and the light in June is a thin thing that grows from the ground and takes the earth with it and lays it bare. After a while he rose and went back out and the moon was up over the eastern hill and it was big and ill-formed at the edge like something not yet finished and it lit the orchard in a way that made the dead leaves look like coins. He stood with his thumbs in his pockets and the dog came and sat and put its head against his shin and he looked down at it. He said. Well.

In the night there came a wind and the wind ran along the ditches and along the rows and it smelt of hearts of weeds and of some small corruption and it brought from a far place the sound of a train and the train climbed some grade where no man could climb without help and the steadiness of it put him in mind of the men who would come one day and carry things out and roll things in and who would not say two words to the house and would not look back when they left. He closed his eyes. The world turned in its old manner. There is a line through all things you can follow if you can keep from lying to yourself and men will hang their lives on it and never even see it and the morning will come whether they have done their work or not and whether their work is worthy of the name.

When sleep came it came quick and without quarters and when he woke again it was as if someone had lifted him and set him down someplace new. The dawn was a faint lint and he went in to the kitchen and Hannah was up already with her hair braided and a cup in her hand and the smell of coffee was there though there was none and would not be. He stood and she set another cup and they watched the light.

Far off in the road a dust rose and drifted and he thought first of Jennisons truck and then of Cal and then of the boy on the motorcycle and last of his own father who had come down that road a thousand times and would never come again. He did not move and neither did she and the light strengthened and reached and took the orchard and held it and showed it for what it was and for what it had been and for what it would be and there is no one who can stand that and say in that moment I am equal to it but a man can put on his hat and go out and lay his hand to the pump and pull down the lever and hear the whine and the catch and feel the small tremor under the soles of his boots that tells him that for the time being water is on the move in the world and taking its own counsel and not his and that will have to be enough and so he did and it was.
