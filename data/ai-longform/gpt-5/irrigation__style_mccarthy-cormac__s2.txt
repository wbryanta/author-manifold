The brother did not shift from it and they walked on and they went slow with the tired and the day gone behind them like a field they had crossed and they had not stopped in the middle of it because the sun was on their necks and there was not a little shade. The realtor's folder was still where it lay on the gate and the wind had taken one sheet and blown it to the fenceline and it lay there like a white animal caught in wire. They did not pick it up.

Down along the creekbed if you could call it that there was a cow skull someone had hung on the post a years back and it had gone to chalk and it faced the orchard and the dead things always do face the living like judges. Beyond it a slow car in the dusk and the sound of its tires like a whisper and the day had ended and something else had called them and they went on and the orchard lay behind them and did not speak. They would return tomorrow and the next and there would be other work and other failures and other men in trucks and the sun would come up again on it as if none of it mattered at all and as if everything did, because those are the only two ways the sun knows to rise. They drove with the windows down and the night air came in and the smell of water from what they'd managed to send into the ground tonight came too, weak but there and they breathed it and did not talk and the crickets opened their small cases and played them and all across the valley men listened and did not know it because they were asleep, and the brothers went up the lane to the house where their mother could be a shape in the window and might not and they would wash at the basin and eat and sit and the day's heat would leave them and the weight would not, and there on the table the card with the man's name would assume its own piece of the wood and claim itself there, and nothing would see fit to remove it because to remove it is to say that what is coming can be delayed at all. They went. The engine made a sound like a big animal that had lived a long time and asked only not to be broken this night, and the front porch light they had put on a timer last winter when the sun went early came on and washed the dust around the step and left dark elsewhere and the orchard the other way was black and held itself like the sea.
