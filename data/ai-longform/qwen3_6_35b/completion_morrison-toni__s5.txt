And when the fire died, and the night grew cold, and the stars moved across the sky, he rose, gathered the satchel, mounted, and rode forward, not because he knew where he was going, but because staying still would have meant surrendering to a fear he had already outlasted once, and because the road, like the law, like the land, like the quiet eyes of a mother drawing water at the edge of the world, did not ask for certainty, only for the willingness to move forward, step by measured step, until the horizon changed shape, or until the breath ran out, or until the silence finally spoke.
