"Do you think it will come?" she asked, her voice so quiet he almost missed it.

"What?" he replied, the word feeling heavy and clumsy in his mouth.

"The warmth. The light that stays."

He did not answer, for there was no answer that would not be a lie, and he could not bring himself to offer her the hollow comfort of a falsehood. He struggled to start a fire, the wood too damp, the effort too great, and eventually, he settled for a small, guttering flame that provided more smoke than heat, casting long, flickering shadows that danced erratically against the stone.

The silence was different now, heavier than it had been in the morning, a silence that was thick with the presence of the night and the weight of the things they could not say.
